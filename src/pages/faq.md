<h2>Buying a Hot Tub</h2>

Stop by our <a href="/about/">Midvale showroom</a> to see models in person and get current pricing.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<h2>Ownership & Costs</h2>

<div class="accordion mb-5">
  <div class="accordion-title"><h3 style="margin:0;font-size:18px;">How much electricity does a hot tub use?</h3></div>
  <div class="accordion-content" style="display:none;">
    <p>Most modern hot tubs cost $20–$50 per month in electricity, depending on the model, insulation quality, climate, and how often you use it. Well-insulated spas with full-foam insulation and quality covers keep costs on the lower end. Utah's cold winters can push costs slightly higher, but a good <a href="/covers/">cover</a> makes a big difference.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="accordion mb-5">
  <div class="accordion-title"><h3 style="margin:0;font-size:18px;">How long do hot tubs last?</h3></div>
  <div class="accordion-content" style="display:none;">
    <p>A quality hot tub typically lasts 15–20 years or more with proper care. The shell, frame, and plumbing are built to last. Components like pumps, heaters, and covers may need replacement over time, but those are straightforward repairs. We service everything we sell.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="accordion mb-5">
  <div class="accordion-title"><h3 style="margin:0;font-size:18px;">How hard is hot tub maintenance?</h3></div>
  <div class="accordion-content" style="display:none;">
    <p>Easier than most people expect. Weekly maintenance takes about 10–15 minutes: test the water, add sanitizer, and clean the filter. You'll drain and refill the tub every 3–4 months. We carry supplies and can walk you through everything when you pick up your spa.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<h2>Installation & Setup</h2>

<div class="accordion mb-5">
  <div class="accordion-title"><h3 style="margin:0;font-size:18px;">Do I need a special electrical hookup?</h3></div>
  <div class="accordion-content" style="display:none;">
    <p>Most full-size hot tubs require a dedicated 240V/50-amp circuit installed by a licensed electrician. Some smaller models — like the <a href="/spa-models/nordic-all-in-110v/">Nordic ALL-IN-110V series</a> — plug into a standard 110V household outlet, making setup much simpler.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="accordion mb-5">
  <div class="accordion-title"><h3 style="margin:0;font-size:18px;">Where should I put my hot tub?</h3></div>
  <div class="accordion-content" style="display:none;">
    <p>A level, solid surface is key — a concrete pad, reinforced deck, or paver patio. Make sure the spot can handle the weight (a filled hot tub with occupants can weigh 3,000–5,000+ lbs). Consider privacy, access to your house, and proximity to the electrical panel. We can help you plan placement.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="accordion mb-5">
  <div class="accordion-title"><h3 style="margin:0;font-size:18px;">Do you deliver and install?</h3></div>
  <div class="accordion-content" style="display:none;">
    <p>Yes. We coordinate delivery and can help with setup and initial water treatment. We'll walk you through everything so you're comfortable using and caring for your new spa from day one.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<h2>Health & Usage</h2>

<div class="accordion mb-5">
  <div class="accordion-title"><h3 style="margin:0;font-size:18px;">Are hot tubs good for your health?</h3></div>
  <div class="accordion-content" style="display:none;">
    <p>Absolutely. Regular hot tub use can relieve stress, ease muscle and joint pain, improve circulation, and promote better sleep. The combination of heat, buoyancy, and hydrotherapy jets provides therapeutic benefits that many owners notice within the first few weeks. Learn more on our <a href="/health-wellness/">health & wellness page</a>.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="accordion mb-5">
  <div class="accordion-title"><h3 style="margin:0;font-size:18px;">Can I use a hot tub in winter?</h3></div>
  <div class="accordion-content" style="display:none;">
    <p>Yes — winter is many owners' favorite season to soak. Modern hot tubs are fully insulated and maintain temperature efficiently even in Utah winters. A good cover keeps heat in and snow out. There's nothing quite like sitting in 102°F water while it's snowing.</p>
  </div>
</div>
